Typography

The best design is invisible; beautiful typesetting often is. Keep it simple and readable like the text in your favorite novel. Avoid using bold, italic, underline, highlights, size changes, bullets, numbering and any other method used to make content stand out more. Those typographic treatments can hinder the reader and add visual clutter to your future design.

Our typefaces

Two main typefaces have been chosen for the CDLE brand. Both have a complement of weights. A third should be used for code.

Novecento

Novecento is a beautiful modern all-caps san-serif font with even coloring and a great selection of weights. It should mainly be used in the logo.

Open Sans

Open Sans has a large x-height making it very easy to read. It is a Google font, which means you can add it to any of your websites or even download it for print use.

Courier New

Courier New is the preferred typeface for displaying code.

Size

Make your copy a comfortable size to read. If you have a ton of copy, you may want to do everything you can to trim it down.

Headlines should be no larger than around 40 pixels.

Body copy should be around 18-21 pixels for comfortable readability across most devices with a line-height roughly 1.4 times the font size. For smaller legal copy and copyright information, going as small as 13-15 pixels is just fine. Again, the line height should be roughly 1.4 times the font size.

Hierarchy

Establishing the order of importance allows a user to quickly find what they are looking for. The headers help determine that hierarchy. There are six in order of most important to least important: h1, h2, h3, h4, h5, h6

There should only be one h1 per article or section. For example, on this website, the logo itself is the h1 and the section headers are h2.

Fallbacks

When using web fonts, it is always a good idea to specify fallback fonts. Fallbacks are typefaces that are common system fonts. If a browser doesn’t support a font, it will move on to the next font in the list.

Here are examples with the preferred fallback order for Open Sans and Courier New respectively:

body {
    /* This is the preferred fallback order for Open Sans */
    font-family: "Open Sans", Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif;
}

code,
pre {
    /* This is the preferred fallback order for Monospace fonts */
    font-family: 'courier new', monospace;
}

Do's and Don'ts

  • Use proper content heirarchy. Be very aware of the visual hierarchy of the information and determine the order one would read it in. Divide your text into header text (usually the name of a page, the title of a section, or title of a document) and body copy (anything in normal paragraphs).
  • Use line spacing that is around 1.4 times the size of the font.
